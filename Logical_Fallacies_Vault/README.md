## Categories

### [[Formal Fallacy]]
Errors in the logical structure of an argument. If the premises are true, the conclusion must be true in a valid argument—but formal fallacies break this rule.

### [[Informal Fallacy]]
Errors in reasoning that stem from content, language, or context rather than logical form. The largest category of fallacies.

### [[Propositional Fallacy]]
Errors that concern compound propositions and their logical connectives.

### [[Quantification Fallacy]]
Errors where the quantifiers of premises contradict the quantifier of the conclusion.

### [[Syllogistic Fallacy]]
Errors that occur in syllogisms—deductive arguments with two premises and a conclusion.

### [[Modal Fallacy]]
Errors involving confusion over what is necessary or possible.

### [[Probabilistic Fallacy]]
Errors in reasoning about probability, statistics, and chance.
